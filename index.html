What is KidniFy?

Discover KidniFy, the ultimate kidney health app. KidniFy combines machine learning, deep learning, and IoT to offer early CKD intervention. Assess your CKD risk, track your health stage, analyze medical images, get personalized diet plans, and check water quality with KidnyPure - all in one app. Take control of your kidney health and enjoy a healthier life with KidniFy.

What do we provide ?

CKD Risk Prediction

Get personalized risk predictions based on your symptoms

CKD Stage Prediction

Assess your risk level with accurate stage predictions using lab data

CT-Image Analyzer

Gain insights into your kidney health by uploading a CT-scanned image

Diet Plan Assignmnet

Receive a tailored diet plan based on disease stage and lab data to maintain healthy kidneys and prevent CKD

KidniPure-The Water Quality Tester

Test the quality of the water sources that you use and get to know whether it is safe to use or not

Our domain

Literature Survey

The literature survey highlights studies applying machine learning for early CKD diagnosis, developing smartphone-based devices for CKD patient self-testing, systems for water quality monitoring and prediction, and platforms providing CKD stage assessment and diet recommendations. Key techniques include machine learning classifiers, mobile health devices with image analysis, IoT sensors with crowdsourcing, and algorithms to categorize CKD severity. The survey demonstrates growing interest in technology-driven solutions for CKD screening, diagnosis, monitoring and management.

Research Gap

The research gap focuses on improving accuracy, accessibility and localization of CKD prediction tools, adopting newer techniques like CNNs for medical imaging, enhancing CKD dietary management through holistic multimodal strategies, developing specialized water quality monitoring systems tailored for CKD patients' health needs, and integrating advanced IoT and ML techniques. Key gaps include accountable and explainable AI, community engagement for water quality, balancing innovation with affordability and strengthening interdisciplinary collaboration. Bridging these gaps requires comprehensive strategies aligned with public health objectives in the distinct Sri Lankan context.

Research Problem

The main objective is developing a mobile application to transform CKD care and prevention in Sri Lanka. Sub-objectives include predicting CKD risk through ML models, classifying CKD stage from medical images via deep learning, generating personalized diet plans with ML algorithms, and implementing an IoT and ML solution for real-time water quality monitoring. The goals focus on improving patient outcomes, alleviating healthcare burden, and pioneering data-driven healthcare.

Research Objectives

Core problems involve limited access to accurate CKD diagnostic tools, lack of localized ML applications capturing Sri Lanka's context, low public awareness impeding early detection, inadequate medical imaging techniques missing disease progression, absence of tailored dietary protocols and lack of real-time water contamination monitoring systems - jeopardizing CKD patients dependent on safe water.

Methodology

The methodology utilizes ML algorithms to predict CKD risk and stage from clinical data, CNN models for CT scan analysis, ML regression for dietary recommendations and an IoT system with ML for water quality monitoring. Techniques include data collection, preprocessing, model optimization, system integration and rigorous testing. User-centric design focuses on intuitive interfaces and accessibility. Cloud platforms enable effective deployment.
