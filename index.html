Our domain

Introducing a groundbreaking initiative in Sri Lanka to combat Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) with the power of technology. We are developing a user-friendly smartphone application that employs cutting-edge Machine Learning (ML) algorithms for accurate CKD prediction. With 16% of the population affected, early detection is vital, and this app aims to be a game-changer. Patients can estimate their CKD risk, while healthcare professionals can utilize it for early detection and personalized treatment plans. By integrating patient-specific data and advanced ML algorithms, we're boosting prediction accuracy and transforming CKD management. But our impact doesn't stop there. CKD in Sri Lanka is influenced by various factors, including water contamination. We're also pioneering IoT and ML technologies to continuously monitor water quality, providing real-time alerts to kidney patients. This innovation ensures safer water, proactive measures, and empowers rural areas. Together, these innovations promise to revolutionize CKD prediction, management, and water quality monitoring, contributing to a healthier Sri Lankan population.

This research project addressed crucial gaps in the prediction and management of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) in Sri Lanka. It identified limitations in existing machine learning applications for CKD prediction, with a focus on improving accuracy, accessibility, and localization. The study also advocated for the use of advanced technology in medical imaging, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and multimodal image fusion, to enhance CKD diagnosis and monitoring. Additionally, the research introduced a novel approach to CKD dietary management using a Random Forest Regressor model, considering multiple dietary attributes for personalized diet plans. Furthermore, the study identified significant research gaps in water quality monitoring systems, emphasizing the need for specialized solutions tailored to CKD patients. Integrating IoT and machine learning into water quality monitoring was explored to enhance accuracy and efficiency, along with improving data accessibility and timely detection of water quality issues.

In Sri Lanka, Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a prevalent health concern, affecting roughly 17.7% of the population. Early detection and intervention are essential, but significant challenges persist. Limited access to diagnostic tools, particularly in rural areas, results in delayed diagnoses and healthcare disparities. To address these issues, the research explored the potential of machine learning (ML) models for CKD prediction within the unique Sri Lankan context. It investigated various factors influencing model performance, aiming to develop more effective predictive tools. The study also assessed public awareness of CKD risks and symptoms to enhance early detection and treatment. It identified barriers to accessing diagnostic tools and proposed strategies for broader access and improved CKD management. Additionally, the research acknowledged the complexity of kidney disease diagnosis, leading to the development of a mobile app providing personalized dietary advice to CKD patients, facilitating adherence to dietary restrictions. Another critical aspect of the research was the absence of a reliable real-time water quality monitoring system in Sri Lanka, which particularly affects kidney patients.

The objectives of this initiative include developing a robust and effective mobile application to predict a user's kidney health condition, selecting and optimizing an image classification model for accurate CKD stage prediction from CT scans, creating a patient-specific care recommendation system, devising a personalized diet plan for CKD patients based on medical history and real-time health indicators, and implementing an Internet of Things and Machine Learning-based water quality monitoring system tailored to kidney patients' needs. The central aim is to enhance the well-being of kidney patients by minimizing the risks associated with compromised water sources. This comprehensive approach promises to set new standards for data-driven healthcare and improve patient care in the region.
